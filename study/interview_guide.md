# Interview Guide — Grassroots Tactics AI

Semi-structured. Aim for ~15 minutes per participant after they finish the SUS.
Record audio (with consent). Transcribe later for thematic coding.

## Before starting

- Confirm consent and recording is OK.
- Remind: there are no right or wrong answers; we want honest reactions.
- Restate participant's role briefly (coach / player / spectator).

## Warm-up (2 min)

1. How long have you been involved with football, and in what role?
2. Have you ever used any kind of analysis tool — even something simple like
   reviewing a phone-recorded match with teammates? What did you use and why?

## Reactions to the clip + AI (8 min)

3. **Walk me through what you just saw.** What stood out from the clip itself,
   before we talk about the AI?
4. The AI gave you a headline, an explanation of why it matters, and a
   coaching cue. Which of those three was most useful, and which was least?
   Why?
5. Was there anything in the AI's explanation that **you didn't see** in the
   clip yourself? Did it feel like new information or like the AI was
   making something up?
6. Was there anything that **you noticed but the AI missed**?
7. The system uses "convex hull area" and "centroid distance" — terms you
   never see in the UI. The AI translates these into language like "shape"
   and "compactness". Did the translation feel natural? Where did it
   sound like a robot?

## Trust and adoption (3 min)

8. If a junior coach on your team showed you this tool, would you
   recommend they use it? What would have to change for you to say yes?
9. Where would this fit in your week — pre-training, post-match review,
   half-time, somewhere else, or nowhere?
10. Imagine the cost was zero and it ran on your phone. What would still
    stop you from using it?

## Wrap-up (2 min)

11. If you could change one thing about this system, what would it be?
12. Is there anything about this experience you want to add that I
    didn't ask?

---

## Probes (use as needed)

- "Can you say more about that?"
- "What do you mean by [term]?"
- "Can you give me a specific example from the clip?"
- "How did that compare to what you expected?"

## Coding plan (post-hoc)

Inductive thematic coding (Braun & Clarke 2006). Two coders code the
same 2 transcripts independently, compare, build a codebook, then split
the rest. Compute inter-rater agreement (Cohen's κ) on the shared 2.

Expected theme buckets (NOT a substitute for inductive coding — just a
sanity check that we cover them):
- Trust and credibility of the AI
- Clarity and language fit
- Workflow integration
- Missing or misleading content
- Comparison with current practice
